Issues

  • The current education system within the United States does not adequately cover the entire population.
  • The system as it stands is not able to support the segment of the population that is below average in education.
  • Due to the failure within the current system, and the current standard of having a college degree, there is a distinct lack of social mobility for those affected by the failures of the system. As such the problem is perpetuated and should be addressed.

Justification
  • College costs have been rising faster than wages, even when adjusted for inflation. As such the cost of college is prohibitive for those in lower income brackets.
  • Those who earn a GED instead of a high school degree will earn less, even after receiving a college education.
  • Those with lower educations are less likely to be employed.
Policy Changes
  • Create a stipend for students to finish their high school education. However, there must be a balance between enticing a student to finish school and abusing the system as a means of income.
  • Introduce legislation preventing wage discrimination for employees who have a GED.
Outreach

  • Preformed Message

  • Dear Secretary DeVos, I believe that there are fundamental issues with our education system as it stands right now. After being presented with information regarding the income of students who received GED’s compared to those who had the opportunity to finish high school the wage gap between the groups is appalling. Multiple studies by the United States government have highlighted the inequalities between students, and how these inequalities create a barrier when trying to move up within society. I urge you to take a critical look at education within impoverished communities and consider revamping school districts to help minimize education inequality because of income.
